Five patterns that keep capable leaders stuck

◇
The Over-Prepared Strategist
"How visible can I be?"
You see the system before it's ready. You're often more prepared than leadership — and that clarity feels dangerous to show.
○
The Over-Relied-On Stabilizer
"How much can I carry?"
You're the one everyone leans on. You hold things together — but reliability has quietly replaced leadership, and you're running out of capacity.
△
The System Corrector
"How hard should I push?"
You inherit broken systems and fix them fast. But the pace of your correction outstrips what people can absorb — and you risk becoming the villain.
⬡
The Expansive Optimizer
"What should I not pursue?"
You have too many good paths and no reason to prune. The abundance feels like possibility — but it's quietly becoming paralysis.
□
The Reflective Builder
"Where do I start?"
You think deeply and see clearly — but insight alone isn't creating momentum. You need structure, not more reflection.

The pattern has a name.
The name changes the decision.

Every leader carries a default pattern — a way they respond to pressure, navigate power, and make decisions under uncertainty. The problem is rarely the decision itself. It's the pattern underneath it that you can't see.

When you name the pattern, you change how you approach the decision. That's where clarity starts.
